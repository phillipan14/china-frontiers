Program Design
Overview
A carefully sequenced journey from university research (Beijing) through real-world robotics applications (Shanghai & Hangzhou) to hardware manufacturing (Shenzhen) — with a focus on how robots are improving everyday life.
◎

Mission

Build lasting relationships between American and Chinese young leaders while exploring how robotics and automation are transforming everyday life — from the household to the hospital to the factory floor.

⊡

Cohort

40 fellows — 20 American, 20 Chinese — from high school seniors through early/mid-career professionals (ages 17–30). Selected for curiosity, leadership, and commitment to cross-cultural exchange.

◇

Structure

Core track: Robotics & Intelligent Machines (all fellows). Elective modules: Biotech, Manufacturing, Fintech, or Climate Tech — choose add-on days per city.

△

Route

Beijing (12 days) → Shanghai (11 days) → Hangzhou (8 days) → Shenzhen (11 days). Connected by high-speed rail. Sequenced from research to applications to manufacturing.

⟡

Exchange Model

Fellows are paired 1:1 (American ↔ Chinese) for the full program. Joint workshops, bilingual panels, team capstone projects, and shared housing.

✦

Capstone

Mixed teams of 4 develop and pitch a cross-border venture concept on the final day in Shenzhen, judged by a panel of founders and investors.

Operations
Logistics & Practical Notes

Visa Requirements

  • US citizens need Chinese visa — F-type (exchange) recommended
  • Requires invitation letter from Chinese partner org
  • Processing: 4–7 business days, ~$185
  • Partner with Tsinghua or similar university for invitation letters
  • Chinese participants register with local police within 24h of hotel changes

Accommodation

  • Twin-share rooms (20 rooms/city). Pair American + Chinese as roommates
  • Book Chinese brands: Atour (亚朵), Ji Hotel (全季), Orange (桔子)
  • Clean, modern, well-located — not backpacker hostels
  • Alternative: university guest houses (cheaper, less reliable)

Payments & Money

  • China is almost entirely cashless — Alipay and WeChat Pay
  • Alipay now accepts foreign credit cards — set up before arrival
  • Visa/Mastercard NOT reliable for daily use
  • Program carries ¥20,000 cash as emergency backup

Internet & VPN

  • Google, Instagram, X, WhatsApp are blocked in China
  • Provide Chinese SIM cards + VPN recommendation
  • Group comms via WeChat (essential in China)
  • Pre-departure: download WeChat, Alipay, DiDi, Baidu Maps

Health & Safety

  • All four cities are very safe (among lowest violent crime globally)
  • Summer heat: up to 35°C+ in SH/HZ/SZ. Schedule outdoors for mornings
  • Air quality: check AQI daily, provide N95s for AQI > 150
  • International clinics in each city (e.g., United Family Hospital)

Planning Timeline

  • T-6 months: Secure Chinese partner, begin company outreach
  • T-4 months: Open applications for American fellows
  • T-3 months: Select cohort, begin visa process
  • T-2 months: Confirm visits, book hotels & trains
  • T-1 month: Pre-departure orientation (virtual)

Potential Partner Organizations

  • Schwarzman Scholars (Tsinghua) — alumni network, venue access
  • US-China Exchange Foundation (USCEF) — funding, institutional support
  • National Committee on US-China Relations — credibility, government ties
  • Local American Chambers of Commerce (AmCham) in each city
  • Chinese People's Association for Friendship with Foreign Countries
  • China-United States Exchange Foundation — people-to-people focus
